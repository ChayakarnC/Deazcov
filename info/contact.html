Rights and Contact Numbers

For people infected with COVID-19:

According to the announcement of the Ministry of Public Health on July 1, 2022, the public health care system will return to normal. When a person is infected with COVID, medical treatment according to the existing right of treatment is provided free of charge. The service is at a hospital near home. If the doctor considers that it is necessary to be referred to another hospital, the assessment system will be implemented.

Gold Card/30-baht Scheme

  • COVID can be treated at Public Health Service Centers, Warm Community Clinics, Primary Care Units of hospitals according to the 30-baht policy, registered hospitals, drugstores in the program "See, Give, End."
  • Treatment procedure: If the symptoms are not severe (Green patients), patients can pick up essential medicines at a drugstore in the program "See, Give, End" near home. The pharmacy will let patients add its Line to send the ATK test result along with the national ID card, as well as collecting the patient’s medical records and advising medication use. The patient should not take the drug by himself. They should have relatives or friends come to pick up the medicine instead. The patient is quarantined for 10 days.
  • If the symptoms are severe (Yellow/Red patients), patients go to the hospital according to the 30-baht policy. For the case of a high-risk group (group 608), patients receive treatment free of charge according to the discretion of the physician. In case of an emergency, patients have the right to be hospitalized free of charge at the nearest hospital.
  • Contact the hotline, call 1669, press 2, OR the NHSO hotline, call 1330. For the general public, press 14. For group 608, bedridden patients, people with disabilities, press 18.

Social Security Rights

  • COVID can be treated at contracted hospitals under the rights of the social security system, all participating hospitals, clinics/pharmacies registered with the NHSO.
  • Treatment procedure: If the symptoms are not severe, patients are able to receive treatment free of charge according to the "See, Give, End” program at the hospital in the social security rights, all public/private hospitals that provide services, and clinics/pharmacies registered with the NHSO all over the country with a 10-day quarantine.
  • If the symptoms that are severe (Yellow/Red patients) worsen or need to be hospitalized, patients must receive treatment at the discretion of the doctor at the hospital according to the social security rights and all public/private hospitals that provide services free of charge. However, if there is an emergency, rights regarding UCEP Plus can be exercised at the nearest medical facility, anywhere, both under the government and private sectors free of charge.
  • Contact the hotline, call 1506, press 6 and press 7.

Civil Servant Rights

  • All government hospitals

In the case of a high-risk group (group 608) or having severe symptoms,

patients receive free-of-charge treatment at the physician’s discretion (whether it is inpatient treatment or the doctor ordered to treat it at home (Home Isolation)).

***Get treatment at a regular service unit or primary care unit according to the 30-baht policy.

***Group 608 is elderly people over 60 years old, patients with 7 chronic diseases, chronic respiratory disease, cardiovascular disease, chronic renal disease, stroke, obesity, cancer, diabetes, and pregnant women.

***Critical/severe (Red patients) symptoms including extreme tiredness, slurred speech, chest tightness, chest pain, severe pneumonia, fatigue, slow reflexes, unconsciousness, shock/coma, lethargy, fever above 39°C, oxygen value below 94 can utilize the UCEP. Emergency critical illness is eligible to use the rights anywhere free of charge, admitting to the nearest hospital.

***NHSO hotline service 1330

  • COVID-19 positive, no need to call the hotline NHSO 1330.
  • But patients are able to call to ask for the procedure.
  • Or if symptoms worsen and patients want to coordinate to find a bed for treatment in the hospital, call 1330.

If you have any questions, you can call hotline 1669 press 2 or NHSO hotline 1330 (Press 14 or the general public. Press 18 for group 608, bedridden patients, and people with disabilities).
